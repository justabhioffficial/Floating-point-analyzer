Floating Point Theory

Floating-point arithmetic is the most pervasive means of approximating real-world continuity on discrete digital hardware.

Why do Errors Occur?

Computers represent numbers using fixed binary formats (like IEEE 754). Not all decimal numbers can be perfectly mapped to a binary representation, introducing tiny approximations on nearly every calculation step.

Rounding vs Truncation

  • Rounding: Selecting the nearest representable number to the true mathematical value. This minimizes immediate variance.
  • Truncation: Indiscriminately dropping precision beyond a certain point. This consistently forces magnitude towards zero in absolute terms, creating bias.

Error Metrics

Absolute Error: The bare mathematical variance between truth and approximation: |exact - approx|.

Relative Error: Absolute variance scaled to the magnitude of the truth: |exact - approx| / |exact|. This dictates "percentage incorrect".
